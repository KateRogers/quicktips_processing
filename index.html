Overview of Data Processing

01

Data Management Process

A data management process is an approach to working with data from the planning of a data exercise to destruction or retention of the data gathered.

No matter the scope of the data exercise, working with data always involves a series of non-linear steps that help us systematically define and plan our goals, gather data, prepare and analyse that data and ultimately use it to drive data-informed actions.

01

The Goal of Data Processing

Data processing refers to the methods for initially collating and manipulating data to meet analytical requirements. It includes cleaning, verifying, validating, transforming and merging data. More simply put, this is where we take the data that we have in its raw, often messy, form and prepare it for analysis.

01

What is Data Verification?

Data verification is the process of checking if the data that we have is in fact the data we need. This often involves investigating the source, data collection methodology and other aspects of the metadata but may also include verify ambiguous data with the data collector themselves. The better the data documentation, the easier it is to verify data.

01

Verifying COVID-19 Data Metadata

We are working with three different datasets, all downloaded from HDX. HDX makes it easy to verify data using the meta fields.

  1. Provenance: is the data authentic?
  2. Source: Who published the data, and where is it stored?; and
  3. Date: When was the data published?

01

What is Data Validation?

Data validation involves automated checks to ensure that the data entered is in the expected format and meaningful. There are many types of data validation ‘checks’:

  1. Format check, e.g. date in the desired format YYYY/MM/DD;
  2. Presence checks to ensure that data has been entered into a field;
  3. range check to see if a value falls within the specified range, e.g. the number of confirmed cases is positive; and
  4. type check to ensure the correct data type has been entered, e.g. confirmed cases be a number.

01

Validating COVID19 Data

If you download the John Hopkins University data from HDX and open it in Excel (or other spreadsheet software), you are likely to get error notifications. Excel is automatically doing a format check and flagging that the dates in the column headers are not normalised. Our first step will then be to ensure that the dates for each column are in the desired date format, in this case YYYY-MM-DD

01

Data Cleaning

Data cleaning is the process of correcting and deleting inaccurate records from spreadsheets or databases. Broadly speaking data cleaning consists of identifying and replacing incomplete, inaccurate, irrelevant, or otherwise problematic (‘dirty’) data.

01

Cleaning COVID-19 Data

Sticking with our COVID-19 John Hopkins University Data, when we screen for errors there are a few things that jump out. For example, there is subnational data for certain countries but not others, the diamond princess cruise ship is reported as a country and, because we only care about countries with an humanitarian response plan, there is a lot of data irrelevant for our analysis.

After making sure to back up the original data, we can make a copy and delete all of this irrelevant data. This will make subsequent steps simpler.

Understand the Data

02

Before you start, get to know your data

Before digging into a COVID-19 dataset, it is important to spend a bit of time understanding what can and cannot be said based on the data. Too often we jump into analysis before taking the time to understand what aspects of the world our data represents.

The first step is to understand the definitions of the data included in your dataset. To do this, you’ll want to review the following:

  1. the metadata
  2. the data dictionary
